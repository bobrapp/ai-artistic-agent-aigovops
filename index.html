01 The gap we're closing

Most artists are brilliant at their craft. The business side is where they're alone.

Pricing a commission. Filing B&O tax. Reading a gallery contract. Sorting through Etsy versus a personal site. Knowing when to form an LLC. Finding affordable health insurance as a self-employed potter.

None of this gets taught in art school. A CPA charges $200 an hour. A consultant in Seattle is a 90-minute drive. Most artists piece it together from blog posts and friends who learned the hard way.

This project closes that gap with something cheaper and more available than any of those: a knowledgeable advisor who knows Kittitas County, Washington tax law, and the realities of a working art practice — and answers any question, the moment you have it.

"I sold $14,000 of pottery last year and have absolutely no idea what to do about taxes. My CPA charges more than I make in a weekend at Buskers."

03 A typical conversation

It asks the right questions, then gives you the right answer.

This isn't a static FAQ. The coach figures out your medium, your sales channels, and your stage — then gives you advice that fits.

  • Knows Washington's B&O tax, sales tax rates by city, and UBI requirements

  • Tells you when you've hit the limit of what a coach can do — and who locally to call
  • Multilingual on day one (English & Spanish; more on request)
kittitas-coach.org
You
I sold about $14k of pottery last year — mostly at the Kittitas County Fair and a few commissions. Do I need an LLC?
Coach
Probably not yet, but let's check. A few quick questions: Do you sell anything that someone could injure themselves on (heavy ceramics, food-safe glazes)? Are you renting a studio outside your home? And — are you also teaching workshops on the side?

The honest answer for most potters at $14k is: sole proprietor with good insurance beats an LLC. An LLC costs $200/year in WA and protects assets you may not have yet. But there's a tax angle (S-corp election around $40k+) we should revisit when you grow. Want to walk through Module 3 together?

06 Built on local ground

The coach knows our county — by name.

This isn't a generic chatbot trained on national blog posts. The coach is configured with the institutions, professionals, venues, and resources that actually exist in Kittitas County.

When it tells you about a consignment program, it means Gallery One. When it tells you where to apply for a state grant, it means ArtsWA — and the deadlines specific to our region.

As the Arts Commission vets more local CPAs, attorneys, framers, insurance brokers, and printers, they're added to the directory. Over time, the coach becomes a richer map of what's available within a 90-minute drive of Ellensburg.
