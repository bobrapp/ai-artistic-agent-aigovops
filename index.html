01 The gap we're closing

Most artists are brilliant at their craft. The business side is where they're alone.

Pricing a commission. Filing B&O tax. Reading a gallery contract. Sorting through Etsy versus a personal site. Knowing when to form an LLC. Finding affordable health insurance as a self-employed potter.

None of this gets taught in art school. A CPA charges $200 an hour. A consultant in Seattle is a 90-minute drive. Most artists piece it together from blog posts and friends who learned the hard way.

This project closes that gap with something cheaper and more available than any of those: a knowledgeable advisor who knows Kittitas County, Washington tax law, and the realities of a working art practice — and answers any question, the moment you have it.

You
I sold about $14k of pottery last year — mostly at the Kittitas County Fair and a few commissions. Do I need an LLC?
Coach
Probably not yet, but let's check. A few quick questions: Do you sell anything that someone could injure themselves on (heavy ceramics, food-safe glazes)? Are you renting a studio outside your home? And — are you also teaching workshops on the side?

The honest answer for most potters at $14k is: sole proprietor with good insurance beats an LLC. An LLC costs $200/year in WA and protects assets you may not have yet. But there's a tax angle (S-corp election around $40k+) we should revisit when you grow. Want to walk through Module 3 together?

10 Common questions

You're probably wondering a few things.

Is this an AI art generator?
No. This coach never generates, modifies, or evaluates artwork. It's a business advisor — taxes, contracts, pricing, marketing, grants. It fights for artists, not against them.
Can I trust AI for tax advice?
The coach is configured with Washington state tax rules, current rates, and IRS guidelines. It tells you when a question is beyond its scope and points you to a vetted local CPA. Think of it as a well-informed first stop, not a replacement for professional advice.
Is my information private?
Yes. Conversations are private. The coach doesn't share your data with other artists, the Arts Commission, or third parties. You control what you share.
What does it cost?
Nothing. The pilot is completely free for participating artists. It's funded by the Kittitas County Arts Commission as a public service to our local arts community.
I'm not very tech-savvy. Can I still use this?
If you can text a friend, you can use the coach. It's a simple chat interface — type your question in plain English (or Spanish), and get a plain-English answer. No apps to download, no accounts to create.
Do I have to be in Ellensburg?
No — anywhere in Kittitas County. Ellensburg, Cle Elum, Roslyn, Thorp, Kittitas, Vantage. The coach knows the whole county. You just need to be a working artist, maker, performer, or creative based here.
What if it gives me wrong advice?
The coach cites its sources, shows its reasoning, and flags uncertainty. For high-stakes decisions (signing contracts, filing taxes), it recommends confirming with a professional — and connects you to local ones.
How is this different from ChatGPT?
ChatGPT is a general assistant. This coach is configured specifically for Kittitas County artists — it knows Gallery One, Buskers in the Burg, Washington B&O tax rates, ArtsWA deadlines, and 50+ local resources by name. Context is everything.
