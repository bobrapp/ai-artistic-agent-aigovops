# Module 13 — Selling Online

**Purpose:** Help the artist choose the right online sales channels — Etsy, their own store, art-specific marketplaces — and run the economics with eyes open.
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.
**Prerequisites:** Module 04 (Pricing) and Module 10 (Website) helpful.

---

## When to pull this module

- Artist is launching their first online shop
- Artist is on Etsy and wondering if it's worth it
- Artist is choosing between Etsy and their own Shopify
- Artist asks about Saatchi, Artfinder, Singulart
- Artist is shipping art for the first time

---

## Opening (60 seconds)

> *"Selling online is a different game than selling in person. The buyer can't touch the piece; you can't read the room. Photos do 80% of the selling. Shipping is your biggest risk. And every platform takes a different cut. Let's figure out where you should be — and not be."*

---

## Diagnostic questions

1. **What are you selling — originals, prints, or both?**
2. **What price range?** ($30 prints? $3,000 paintings? Wildly different platforms.)
3. **How comfortable are you with shipping?**
4. **How much volume do you want — a few sales a month or many?**
5. **Do you want repeat buyers or one-time sales?**

## Channel #1: Etsy

**The math:**
- Listing fee: $0.20 per item, every 4 months.
- Transaction fee: 6.5% of sale.
- Payment processing: ~3% + $0.25.
- **Effective Etsy cut: ~10% per sale.**
- Plus optional "Etsy Ads" you can opt into (or not).

**Best for:**
- Prints, smaller pieces, jewelry, ceramics under $300.
- Artists with high volume and a "production line" feel.
- Artists who want discoverability without building their own audience.

**Bad for:**
- High-priced originals ($500+).
- Artists who hate the Etsy aesthetic (crafty, busy thumbnails).
- Artists who want a relationship with buyers (Etsy owns the customer).

**Reality check:**
- Etsy buyers expect Etsy prices. Underpriced art is the norm.
- Etsy SEO is its own game. New listings sink unless you spend hours on tags/keywords.
- Etsy can suspend your shop with little warning. **You don't own the channel.**

---

## Channel #2: Your own Shopify (or Squarespace shop)

**The math:**
- Shopify: $39/mo (Basic plan). Or use Squarespace shop ($23/mo, already part of your website cost).
- Payment processing: ~2.9% + $0.30.
- **Effective cut: ~3–4% per sale, but you also pay a monthly fee.**

**Best for:**
- Higher-priced originals.
- Artists building a brand and direct relationships.
- Artists who want full control over presentation.
- Artists with their own audience driving traffic (newsletter, Instagram).

**Bad for:**
- Artists with no audience yet — you'll have a beautiful empty store.

**Honest truth:** Your own store works **if you bring the traffic.** Etsy provides traffic (but takes a cut + owns the buyer). Your store is direct (but you have to market it).

## Channel #3: Art-specific marketplaces

### Saatchi Art

- Cut: ~35% commission.
- Best for: Original paintings, drawings, sculpture, photography.
- Pros: International collector base, free to list.
- Cons: High commission, very crowded.

### Artfinder

- Cut: 35–45% commission (varies by tier).
- Best for: Original art at the mid-tier price.
- Pros: Active buyer base, decent platform.
- Cons: Vetted entry (you apply), commission stings.

### Singulart, Rise Art, etc.

- Generally curated, take 30–50%, target collectors.
- Worth applying if you have a coherent body of work and decent CV.

**Reality:** These platforms can work, but the commission is gallery-level. Treat them like a gallery you don't physically visit.

---

## Channel #4: Your Instagram with DMs

- Some artists effectively sell entirely through Instagram DMs.
- Pros: No platform fee beyond Stripe/PayPal/Venmo.
- Cons: No buyer protection, easy to lose track of sales, taxes still apply.
- Workable for low-volume direct sales; not scalable.

## The honest mix for most Kittitas artists

A reasonable starting setup:

- **Your own Squarespace shop** for originals and a flagship print collection.
- **Etsy** for an "outlet" line of smaller prints, postcards, or affordable pieces.
- **One curated marketplace** (Saatchi or Artfinder) if you're showing originals at the mid-tier.
- **Newsletter** doing the heavy lifting of telling people what's new (see Module 11).

## Shipping art — the part nobody wants to talk about

**Risk reality:** ~3–5% of shipped art arrives damaged or doesn't arrive. You need a system.

### For prints (most common)

- Flat-mailer envelopes for small prints.
- Rigid mailers for medium prints.
- Tubes for larger prints.
- USPS Priority Mail or UPS Ground.
- Always include "Do Not Bend" and tracking.

### For framed work / originals

- Box with 2" of rigid foam on all sides.
- Glassine paper protecting the surface.
- Bubble wrap, kraft paper.
- For glass: tape an "X" across glass to contain shards if it breaks.
- Ship UPS Ground or USPS Priority. **Insure for full value.**

### For sculpture, 3D, fragile

- Custom crate or double-box.
- Foam custom-cut to the piece (Uline foam sheets).
- Get a quote from UPS Pack & Ship if shipping over $500 in value.

### Insurance

- USPS / UPS coverage is limited and they fight claims for "art."
- Consider **third-party shipping insurance** (e.g., Shipsurance) for pieces >$500.
- Or use **a fine art shipper** (UOVO, FineArtShippers) for anything >$2,000 — expensive but reliable.

### Cost rule of thumb

- Free shipping is a sales tool but costs you margin. Build it into the price.
- Or charge shipping at cost + a small handling fee. Be transparent.
- Either way: **don't underprice the piece to subsidize shipping you didn't account for.**

## Sales tax for online sales

WA destination-based rules (covered in Module 07):
- **WA buyer:** collect sales tax at their destination rate.
- **Out-of-state buyer:** generally no WA sales tax. Other state nexus rules may apply if you hit thresholds ($100K in sales to OR/CA/etc., or many transactions).
- **Etsy, Saatchi, Shopify Tax:** often collect and remit on your behalf for marketplace sales. **Verify with each platform what they handle vs. what you handle.**

## Action items

1. Decide your channel mix based on your medium and price range.
2. Set up shipping templates (small prints, large prints, originals) before your first sale.
3. Test-ship a piece to a friend in another state. Note what broke.
4. Add a "Shipping Policy" page to your website.
5. Confirm which platforms remit sales tax for you.

---

## Escalation triggers

- Artist is doing **international sales** at volume → customs, VAT, multi-currency; consultant or platform that handles this.
- Artist is **damaged in shipping** repeatedly → professional fine art shipper.
- Artist is hit with **sales tax nexus notice** from another state → CPA or sales tax service.
- Artist is **wholesaling online** to retail buyers → wholesale terms, business credit checks; CPA.
