# Module 07 — Washington State Taxes for Artists

**Purpose:** Walk the artist through B&O tax, sales tax, and local taxes — the WA-specific obligations most artists get wrong.
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.
**Prerequisites:** Module 02 (Registering) — they should have a UBI.

---

## When to pull this module

- Artist asks "what's B&O tax?"
- Artist is panicking about a WA DOR letter
- Artist is unsure when to collect sales tax
- Artist is doing their first annual filing
- Artist is selling at a multi-state fair and confused

---

## Opening (60 seconds)

> *"Washington doesn't have state income tax, which is great. But it makes up for it with two business taxes that confuse the hell out of most artists: B&O (gross receipts) and sales tax. The good news — at typical artist revenue, you'll likely owe close to zero in B&O thanks to a small-business credit. Let's go through it."*

---

## Diagnostic questions

1. **Have you filed a WA state return before?**
2. **What's your gross revenue (everything you sold) for the year?**
3. **Do you sell tangible art (paintings, pottery) or services (workshops, lessons), or both?**
4. **Do you sell in person at events, or only online / through galleries?**
5. **Have you ever charged sales tax on a sale?**

---

## Tax #1: B&O (Business & Occupation tax)

**What it is:** A gross-receipts tax. Calculated on what you sold, *not* on profit.

**Two main rates for artists:**

- **Retailing rate:** ~0.471% on retail sales of tangible artwork.
- **Service & other:** ~1.5% on workshops, lessons, commissions for services.

**Example:**
- You sold $20,000 of pottery direct = retailing → $20,000 × 0.471% = **$94 B&O owed**.
- You also taught $5,000 of workshops = service → $5,000 × 1.5% = **$75 B&O owed**.
- Subtotal: **$169 B&O**.

### The Small Business Credit (this is the magic)

WA gives a credit that often wipes out B&O for small operations. For annual filers in 2026 (verify rates):
- If total B&O owed is below ~$300 (annual filer), the credit reduces it.
- If you're well below, you may owe **$0** on B&O.

**You still have to file.** Filing a $0 return is fine; *not* filing triggers fines and license suspension.

---

## Tax #2: Sales tax (the one customers actually see)

You must collect WA sales tax on **retail sales of tangible artwork delivered in WA**.

### When you charge it

- **In-person sales** at studio, fairs, open studios — yes, collect at the rate of where you're delivering (your location for studio; the event location for fairs).
- **Online sales delivered to WA addresses** — yes, at the buyer's destination rate.
- **Online sales delivered out of state** — no (unless you cross another state's nexus threshold).
- **Wholesale to a retailer** (e.g., selling to Gallery One for resale) — no sales tax; they give you their resale certificate.
- **Commissions for services** (a portrait you didn't physically deliver as a tangible piece — like digital files) — generally not subject to sales tax (services aren't taxable in WA, with exceptions).

### Rates by city (destination-based)

- **Ellensburg:** ~8.2% (verify with WA DOR Sales Tax Lookup)
- **Cle Elum:** ~8.0%
- **Unincorporated Kittitas County:** ~7.7%
- **Other Kittitas County cities** (Kittitas, Roslyn, South Cle Elum): rates vary slightly

**Use the WA DOR sales tax lookup tool** (dor.wa.gov) for every address-based sale. Rates change annually.

### How to handle it

1. Add sales tax to the buyer's total. *Don't* absorb it into your price.
2. Track sales tax collected separately in your bookkeeping.
3. Remit to WA DOR when you file your B&O return.

---

## Tax #3: Local city taxes

- **Ellensburg:** No additional city B&O tax (some cities have one; Ellensburg doesn't, as of 2026). City business license required.
- **Cle Elum:** Same — local business license, no separate city B&O.

---

## Filing — the actual mechanics

You file through **MyDOR** (dor.wa.gov):

1. Log in.
2. Select your tax period.
3. Enter gross revenue, broken down by category (retailing vs. service).
4. Enter sales tax collected, broken by location code.
5. Apply small-business credit if eligible.
6. Pay any balance due (or confirm $0).

**Time:** 20 minutes once you know what you're doing.

**Frequency:** WA DOR assigns based on revenue. Most artists are **annual** ($0–$28K), **quarterly** ($28K–$200K), or **monthly** ($200K+).

---

## The reseller permit reminder

If you have one (Module 02 covered it), use it when buying materials wholesale. You give the supplier your reseller permit number, and they don't charge you sales tax. You'll collect tax when you sell the finished piece.

**Caution:** Don't use the reseller permit for studio tools (kiln, easels, computer) — those aren't being resold. Pay sales tax on those normally.

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Not filing because you owe $0.** Triggers penalties and a "non-filer" status with DOR. Always file.
2. **Charging the wrong sales tax rate.** Cle Elum's rate isn't Ellensburg's; the buyer's delivery address determines it.
3. **Pocketing sales tax** as if it were revenue. It's not yours — it's the state's.
4. **Forgetting to add sales tax** to the price at a fair. Now it's coming out of your pocket.
5. **Filing under the wrong category.** Retailing rate is 0.471%; service rate is 1.5%. Mis-categorizing workshops as retailing under-pays B&O.

---

## Kittitas County specifics

- **Buskers in the Burg** and the **Kittitas County Fair**: organizers often handle a temporary sales tax registration but verify with them. You're still responsible for collecting and remitting.
- **Selling at the Cle Elum Farmers Market or Suncadia events**: charge the Cle Elum rate (~8.0%).
- **Suncadia and area hotels** purchasing for guest rooms: these are retail sales — collect sales tax. (Unless they have a resale certificate, which is rare for hospitality.)

---

## Action items

1. Log into MyDOR and confirm your filing frequency.
2. Bookmark the WA DOR Sales Tax Rate Lookup tool.
3. Add a sales-tax-tracking column to your bookkeeping if you don't have one.
4. Calendar your filing due date — annual is April 15; quarterly is the end of the month after the quarter.
5. Verify city of Ellensburg / Cle Elum business license is current.

---

## Escalation triggers

- Artist is selling at fairs in **other states** (Oregon, Idaho) → multi-state nexus; consult CPA or use sales tax software.
- Artist received a **WA DOR audit notice** → CPA or tax attorney.
- Artist owes **back B&O for multiple years** → CPA; voluntary disclosure may reduce penalties.
- Artist is **wholesaling significantly** → confirm reseller certificate handling with CPA.
