# Module 14 — Art Fairs & Festivals

**Purpose:** Help the artist evaluate, apply to, and actually profit from art fairs and festivals — including the local Kittitas County circuit.
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.
**Prerequisites:** Module 04 (Pricing) and Module 07 (WA Taxes) helpful.

---

## When to pull this module

- Artist is considering their first fair
- Artist applied to several fairs and is choosing which to do
- Artist did a fair, lost money, and wants to understand why
- Artist is doing Buskers in the Burg or the County Fair for the first time

---

## Opening (60 seconds)

> *"Fairs are a strange business: long days, physical labor, weather, and you might net $200 or $5,000 from the same booth depending on the event, the weather, and what you brought. Done well, fairs build your audience and pay your rent. Done badly, they drain your inventory for a sunburn. Let me walk you through making them work."*

---

## Diagnostic questions

1. **Have you done fairs before?** (Which ones; how did they go?)
2. **What's your medium and price range?**
3. **Do you have inventory built up, or are you producing for the fair?**
4. **Do you have a booth setup?** (Tent, grids, lighting, display.)
5. **What's your goal — sales, audience, or both?**

## The math of a fair

**Costs:**
- **Booth fee:** $50 (small local) to $1,500 (major juried art fair).
- **Travel/lodging** if it's away from home.
- **Display setup amortized:** A decent tent + grid walls + lighting setup costs $1,500–3,500; amortize over 5+ fairs/year.
- **Inventory carrying cost:** What you didn't sell sits until next time.
- **Your time:** 2 days minimum (setup + show + breakdown); often 3–4 days.
- **Sales tax** you'll collect (revenue but pass-through).

**Break-even rule of thumb:**

For a fair to be worth your time, you need to net **at least $300/day** above costs after sales tax. Below that, you're working at fast-food wages, paying your own benefits.

**Top-tier fairs** can net $2,000–$10,000+ in a weekend.

## Tiers of fairs (Pacific Northwest)

### Tier A: Major regional juried fairs

- Bellevue Festival of the Arts, Edmonds Arts Festival, Anacortes Arts Festival, Bumbershoot (when it has art).
- Booth fees $400–$900. Heavy juried. High foot traffic.
- Real money is possible. Real rejection is possible.

### Tier B: Local festivals & fairs

- Buskers in the Burg (Ellensburg), Kittitas County Fair, Cle Elum/Roslyn events, Yakima Valley fairs.
- Booth fees $50–$300. Less juried or unjuried. Lower foot traffic but loyal local audience.
- Lower ceiling on sales but lower risk and excellent for building local relationships.

### Tier C: Holiday markets, farmers markets, popup events

- $25–$100 per day. Casual.
- Test new work, sell prints and small pieces, meet other artists.

## What to bring (the inventory mix)

A fair booth needs a price ladder so every visitor finds something they can afford:

- **Impulse buys:** Postcards ($5), magnets, stickers, small prints ($15–35). High volume; pays the booth fee.
- **Mid-tier:** Medium prints, small originals ($75–250). Where most sales happen.
- **Hero pieces:** Larger originals ($500–2,500). They draw people in. Most won't buy these; some will.

**The 5/25/70 rule:**
- 5% of visitors buy a hero piece.
- 25% buy a mid-tier.
- 70% who buy at all buy an impulse item.

Build inventory accordingly.

---

## Booth setup essentials

- **Tent** (10' × 10' minimum, white): $200–800. Trimline or EZ-Up.
- **Grid walls or pro panels:** $300–1,000.
- **Tables, easels, displays:** $200–500.
- **Lighting:** Battery LEDs if no power.
- **Sandbags or weights:** Critical for wind.
- **Square reader** and a charged phone with a backup battery.
- **Cash float:** $100–200 in small bills.
- **Sales sheets, business cards, postcards** for capture.
- **Email signup clipboard** with an obvious incentive.

**Setup time first time:** 2 hours. Second time: 1 hour. After 5 fairs: 30 minutes.

---

## Pre-fair prep

- Apply 3–6 months in advance for juried fairs.
- Confirm whether organizer handles sales tax (Kittitas County Fair usually does; smaller events may not).
- Photograph all inventory before the fair (insurance documentation).
- Bring inventory log.
- Have a cancellation plan if weather is brutal.

---

## On-fair day

**Mindset:** Be present. Smile. Don't sit behind a table on your phone — stand in front of the booth.

**Conversation openers that aren't pushy:**
- "Let me know if you have any questions about the work."
- "These are all made in Ellensburg / Cle Elum."
- "Want to see something from the same series?"

**Closing without pressure:**
- "Want me to wrap that up?"
- "We can do payment plans for the larger pieces if helpful."
- "If you want to think on it, here's a card."

**Capture every email.** A fair with $0 in sales but 40 email captures is still useful.

---

## Sales tax at fairs

- WA fairs: collect sales tax at the **event location's destination rate** (not your studio's rate).
- For Buskers in the Burg or Kittitas County Fair: Ellensburg rate (~8.2%).
- For Cle Elum events: Cle Elum rate (~8.0%).
- Track separately. Remit to WA DOR with your B&O return.
- Many event organizers issue a sub-permit and handle some of this; confirm in writing.

---

## Post-fair routine

- Reconcile sales (cash, Square, Stripe).
- Email new subscribers within 1 week with a "thanks for visiting" + new work / studio update.
- Restock inventory.
- Note what sold and didn't — adjust next time.
- File any sales tax with WA DOR.

## Kittitas County specifics

### Buskers in the Burg (summer, Ellensburg)

- Free-flowing street festival, not a juried art fair.
- Foot traffic favors **impulse buys and mid-tier** pieces over hero pieces.
- Apply through Ellensburg Downtown Association months in advance.
- Booth fees modest. Good for local audience building.

### Kittitas County Fair (Labor Day weekend)

- Multi-day, agricultural-county-fair feel.
- Mixed audience — locals, tourists, families.
- Strong for accessibly-priced work; moderate for fine art originals.
- Apply via the Fair's vendor process in spring.

### Cle Elum-Roslyn area events

- Smaller, seasonal, more curated by chamber and community organizations.
- Good for relationship-building with Upper County audience.

### Regional ladder (within driving distance)

- **Yakima Folklife** (June) — mid-tier festival.
- **Wenatchee Arts Festival** — solid foot traffic.
- **Bellevue Festival of the Arts** (late July) — major juried; worth applying once your work is ready.
- **Edmonds Arts Festival** (June) — major juried; competitive.

---

## Action items

1. List 3–5 fairs you're qualified to apply to in the next 12 months.
2. Calculate booth fee + travel + your time at $300/day minimum to see if each pencils.
3. If you don't have a booth setup, plan one (used setups on Facebook Marketplace are common).
4. Build a "fair inventory" production plan — what to make, by when.
5. Practice your booth setup in your driveway before the first event.

---

## Escalation triggers

- Artist is applying to **multi-state national-tier fairs** → sales tax nexus in each state, may need a sales tax service.
- Artist is doing **30+ fairs/year** → real business operation; CPA + dedicated fair-management strategy.
- Artist has had a **major loss at an event** (theft, damage, weather destroying booth) → insurance claim, possibly attorney.
