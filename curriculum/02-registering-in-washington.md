# Module 02 — Registering Your Business in Washington

**Purpose:** Walk the artist through every registration they need to legally operate in WA and Kittitas County.
**Estimated time:** 30–40 minutes (mostly form-filling).
**Prerequisites:** Module 01 (Hobby or Business?) — they should know they're a business.

---

## When to pull this module

- Artist has decided they're a business and asks "what do I have to do?"
- Artist is about to do their first fair / first gallery show
- Artist got a letter from WA Department of Revenue
- Artist asked about a "business license"

---

## Opening (60 seconds)

> *"Good news: Washington makes this easier than most states. You'll get one number (UBI) that covers most of what you need, and you can do it all online in about an hour. Bring coffee."*

Set expectations: 2–4 weeks until everything is fully active.

---

## Diagnostic questions

1. **What's the legal name you want to use?** (Your own name? A studio name?)
2. **Are you selling tangible goods, services, or both?** (Paintings = goods. Workshops = services. Both = both.)
3. **Will you have any employees in the next 12 months?** (Almost always no for solo artists.)
4. **Will you be selling in person at events?** (Affects sales tax obligations.)
5. **Are you in Ellensburg, Cle Elum, or unincorporated Kittitas County?** (Local licensing differs.)

---

## The five-step checklist

### 1. WA Business License (UBI)

- **Where:** [business.wa.gov](https://business.wa.gov) → Apply for Business License
- **Cost:** $90 initial + $11 trade name registration (if using one)
- **What you get:** A UBI (Unified Business Identifier) — your business's tax ID for WA
- **Time:** ~30 minutes online; license arrives in ~10 days

You'll be asked:
- Business structure (sole prop is fine for now; can change later)
- Business activities (select "Artist" or "Retail Trade" depending)
- Estimated gross revenue
- Whether you'll collect sales tax (yes if selling tangible art to WA buyers)

### 2. Trade Name (DBA) — optional

If your business name is **different from your legal name**, register the trade name. "Jane Smith" doesn't need a DBA. "Stuart Range Ceramics" does. Done on the same form as step 1.

### 3. B&O Tax registration

Automatic when you get the UBI. You'll be assigned a filing frequency (annual, quarterly, or monthly) based on estimated revenue. **Most artists start annual.**

### 4. Reseller Permit — strongly recommended

- **What it does:** Lets you buy materials wholesale without paying sales tax on them (since you'll collect tax when you sell the finished piece).
- **How:** Apply through your MyDOR account after you have a UBI.
- **Note:** Only works for materials that become part of what you sell. Studio tools (a kiln) still get taxed.

### 5. Local city/county license

- **City of Ellensburg:** Business license required. ~$25/year. Apply at City Hall or online.
- **City of Cle Elum:** Business license required. ~$25–50/year. Apply at City Hall.
- **Unincorporated Kittitas County:** Usually no separate license required unless you operate a physical storefront.
- **Selling at events:** Festival organizers often handle a temporary registration on your behalf — ask.

---

## What you don't need (yet)

- **An EIN from the IRS** — only required if you have employees or form an LLC. A sole prop can use their SSN.
- **An LLC** — Module 03 covers when this makes sense.
- **A trademark** — Module 09 covers when this makes sense.

---

## After registration: ongoing obligations

- **WA B&O / sales tax return:** File on your assigned schedule. Annual filers file by April 15. **You must file even if you owe $0.**
- **City of Ellensburg / Cle Elum renewal:** Annually.
- **Federal Schedule C:** Once a year with your 1040.
- **Federal quarterly estimated taxes:** If you'll owe more than $1,000 in federal tax. Module 06 covers this.

---

## Kittitas County specifics

- The **Kittitas County Chamber** can help with the WA forms — free, no appointment usually needed.
- The **CWU Small Business Development Center** offers free advising for new business owners; they're familiar with the artist case.
- For **Buskers in the Burg** and the **Kittitas County Fair**, the organizers handle a temporary vendor registration — you don't need to do it separately.

---

## Common mistakes

1. **Not registering until "you have to."** WA technically requires registration before your first taxable sale. Festival sales count.
2. **Forgetting the reseller permit** and paying sales tax on $2K of materials you didn't have to.
3. **Picking monthly B&O filing** when annual is fine — gives you 11 fewer forms to file.
4. **Using a studio name without registering the DBA.** Your bank may not deposit checks made out to "Stuart Range Ceramics" if you haven't registered it.

---

## Action items

1. Set aside 60 minutes this week. Do the WA business license application.
2. Apply for the reseller permit the day your UBI arrives.
3. Visit Ellensburg / Cle Elum City Hall (or website) for the local license.
4. Open a dedicated business bank account using your UBI.
5. Bookmark MyDOR — you'll log in once a year minimum.

---

## Escalation triggers

- Artist is forming an **LLC** — this changes the order of operations (LLC first with Secretary of State, then UBI). Go to Module 03.
- Artist has **employees** or **independent contractors** they pay >$600 — needs an EIN and additional registrations; CPA helpful.
- Artist is operating across **multiple states** — multi-state nexus questions, CPA needed.
