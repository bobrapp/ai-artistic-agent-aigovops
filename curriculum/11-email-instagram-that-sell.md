# Module 11 — Email & Instagram That Actually Sell

**Purpose:** Help the artist build a marketing rhythm that drives real sales — not vanity metrics. Focus on email (the highest-converting channel) plus a sustainable Instagram practice.
**Estimated time:** 30 minutes.
**Prerequisites:** Module 10 (Portfolio Website) — they should have a place to send people.

---

## When to pull this module

- Artist asks "should I be on TikTok?" or any "which platform?" question
- Artist has followers but no sales
- Artist is overwhelmed by the marketing-content treadmill
- Artist is launching a new collection or show
- Artist asks about email lists

---

## Opening (60 seconds)

> *"Most artists obsess over Instagram followers and ignore the channel that actually sells: email. The artists I see making real money are running an email list with 200 engaged subscribers and ignoring half of social. Let's talk about a marketing rhythm you can actually sustain — and that pays off."*

The reframe: stop creating "content," start building a relationship.

---

## Diagnostic questions

1. **Do you have an email list right now?** (Size?)
2. **Roughly how often do you post on Instagram?**
3. **When was the last time you got a sale or inquiry from social vs. from email vs. from your website directly?**
4. **What feels hardest about marketing right now?**
5. **How much time per week can you sustainably give marketing?** (Honest answer.)

---

## The principle: email > social

- Email open rates for artists: **20–40%**.
- Instagram organic reach: **2–5%** of your followers see any given post.
- Email subscribers self-selected to hear from you. Instagram followers may have followed two years ago for a single piece and never see you again.

**A 200-person email list often outsells a 10,000-follower Instagram.**

---

## The minimum viable email setup

### 1. Pick a platform

- **Mailchimp** — free up to 500 contacts, then ~$13/mo. Familiar interface, good for artists.
- **Buttondown** — $9/mo, clean, writer-friendly, minimal interface.
- **Substack** — free, doubles as a blog, great if you write essays.
- **ConvertKit / Kit** — $15+/mo, more automation, overkill for most.

**Default for most Kittitas artists: Mailchimp** or Buttondown.

### 2. Embed a signup form on your website

- "Studio updates, new work, occasional notes from the kiln. ~1 email a month."
- Be honest about frequency and content.
- Offer a small incentive if you have one: a digital postcard, a behind-the-scenes piece.

### 3. Decide on cadence

Sustainable cadences:
- **Monthly** — most realistic, hard to mess up.
- **Bi-weekly** — only if you have something every two weeks.
- **Weekly** — only for serious operations.

**Pick one and stick to it.** Inconsistent emails train people to ignore you.

### 4. Write emails people open

Format:
- **One image** at the top (your newest piece, or your studio).
- **A short personal note** (~150–300 words). "Here's what I made this month. Here's the story." Voice matters more than polish.
- **One clear call-to-action**: visit the shop, RSVP to open studio, reply with thoughts.

What works:
- Studio process glimpses
- "I made this and I'm not sure about it" vulnerability
- New work announcements with a 48-hour first-look window for subscribers (rewards them for being on the list)
- Show invitations
- Local content: "Heading to Buskers next weekend, come find me at booth 12"

What doesn't:
- "Buy now!" with no context
- Bland updates with no personal voice
- Long emails (they don't get read)

## Instagram as a top-of-funnel

Think of Instagram as the entrance, not the destination. Goal: get people from Instagram → website → email list.

### Sustainable cadence (vs. burnout)

- **3 posts a week** (carousels or single posts of work)
- **2–3 stories a week** (process, daily life, polls)
- **1 Reel a month** if you're inclined (Reels get more reach but more effort)

This is sustainable for most working artists. Don't believe people who tell you to post daily — they're either marketers (different job than yours) or unsustainable.

### Content pillars (rotate among these)

1. **Finished work** — high-quality images, multiple angles, scale context.
2. **Process** — work-in-progress, materials, studio.
3. **Story** — why you made it, what it's about, where you were that day.
4. **Behind-the-scenes** — your studio, your tools, your dog.
5. **Calls-to-action** — show announcements, new collection drops, open studio invites.

### Caption template

- Hook (first line — what would make someone stop scrolling)
- Context (the story behind the piece)
- Detail (medium, dimensions, price or "DM for pricing")
- CTA (something to do — link in bio, comment, save, share)

### Hashtags

Use 5–15 relevant ones. Mix:
- Medium: #ceramics #wheelthrownpottery
- Regional: #pacificnorthwestart #kittitascounty #ellensburgart
- Style: #wabisabi #functionalpottery
- Series-specific

## Local visibility tactics that beat Instagram

- **First Friday Art Walks in Ellensburg** — show up, hand out cards.
- **Daily Record** — write a press release for shows; they often run it.
- **Gallery One newsletter** — ask to be featured.
- **CWU events** — student and faculty events bring potential buyers.
- **Suncadia gallery talks** — area resorts sometimes host artists.
- **Roslyn Theatre lobby exhibitions** — small but engaged audience.

## The 80/20 marketing rhythm

For an artist with **5 hours/week** of marketing time:

- **2 hours/week:** create one quality post (photograph, edit, write caption, schedule)
- **1 hour/week:** stories and engagement (respond to DMs, comment on other artists)
- **1 hour/month:** write and send your email newsletter
- **1 hour/month:** plan next month's content
- **1 hour/month:** local — drop off cards, schedule a Daily Record pitch, visit a gallery

Spend more if you want. **Spend less and you'll be invisible.**

## Action items

1. Set up an email platform this week (Mailchimp free tier is fine).
2. Embed a signup form on your website.
3. Pick a cadence (monthly recommended) and put it in your calendar.
4. Send your first email this month — even if it's short. Done > perfect.
5. Audit your Instagram: are you sustaining or burning out? Adjust.
6. Pick one local visibility tactic this month and do it.

---

## Escalation triggers

- Artist wants **paid ads** (Instagram, Google) → can work but watch ROI carefully; consider a marketing consultant for one strategy session.
- Artist has **mid-sized list (2K+)** and wants advanced automation → ConvertKit/Kit, or a one-time marketing consultant.
- Artist is suffering **clear burnout** from marketing → reduce, don't increase. Maybe drop Instagram entirely.
